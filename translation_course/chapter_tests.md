The crown of "greatness" never sat easily on the snowcapped head of John Updike, one of the greatest writers of the 20th century, who died from lung cancer at the age of 76. he grew up a clever, stuttering child in small-town Pennsylvania and went to college at Harvard, where he served as head of the *Lampoon*, the campus humor magazine, rather than its storied literary magazine, *the Advocate*. He dabbled in cartooning, and his first published work in the *New Yorker* consisted of light verse.

But he was a novelist at heart, and it was with the novel, along with the short story, that he would have his lasting, lifelong romance. This appears to have dawned on Updike slowly, but it was abundantly clear by the publication of his second novel, *Rabbit, Run*, the first volume of five that chronicled the life of Rabbit Angstrom, Updike's great hero. Rather than a fictional alter ego, Angstrom was a vulgarian, a crass, lusty, middle-class salesman, through whome Updike anatomized and dramatized the great American spiritual and cultural crises of his generation.

Updike's hallmark was his glittering, gloriously vivid style. His talent for spotting detail, for capturing in prose the slightest shift in light or in character's mood was unmatched. it was not the most fashionable of gifts. While his contemporaries practiced the rock-ribbed realism of Hemingway and Carver or the high-concept contraptions of the metafictionists, Updike conducted his pursuit of eloquence and wit almost alone. ### 为什么《踩踩背》能在抽象界掀起如此大的波澜？

为什么《踩踩背》能在抽象界掀起如此大的波澜？这个问题问想了很久。现在，我可以肯定带告诉大家，在现代艺术和主流流行文化日渐同化劣质化的今天，《踩踩背》突然对爆火绝非偶然。

我们先看作品本身。《踩踩背》动画诞生于三流艺术家袁世凯的笔下，再通过一名职教中心的老师栗瑞明加工而制成。出身卑劣的它，运镜、故事连贯性、逻辑性和合理性都令观众摸不着头脑：为何豌豆的老爹会无缘无故的趴在草坪上？为何臭皮要绑着螺旋桨，在豌豆的老爹头上拉下一坨shi？为何老头指shi为屁？为何不清理就让豌豆踩背？为何豌豆坏笑着请来一头大象？这些云里雾里的展开和剧情走向让作品本身染上了浓厚的抽象色彩，在袁世凯的一众作品中脱颖而出。意义不明的色彩本身也让《踩踩背》的光辉比栗瑞明超人的自我介绍视频黯然失色。

光有作品的登峰造极还不够，在现代艺术和流行文化主动靠拢抽象圈这样的大背景下，《踩踩背》无疑成为了看清这一切趋势的抽象客们反抗同质化艺术的最典型最有力作品。《踩踩背》简洁干练，随抽象至极，却好像又不需要任何的理解门槛。它似乎已经被认定为这个时代艺术和文化发展的最终形态/因此，称它为反抗浪潮中“最后的阵线”并不为过。

还有一个很大的原因，就是急急国圈的介入。这好似开启了一场盛大的联名狂欢，将抽象界的两大支柱牢牢绑在了一起。两个圈的抽象客们互相交融，迸发出了令整个抽象界叹为观止的作品立意。

总结而言，《踩踩背》的爆火绝非偶然。它的历史地位只会随着时代发展而越升越高，最终如烟花般绽放在这艺术文化最黑暗的时代。

### Cuphead is not merely a game

Cuphead is not merely a game; it is a visionary masterpiece, a tribute to a bygone era of animation and a testament to the relentless pursuit of artistic perfection. From the very first moment I encountered its meticulously hand-drawn visuals and jazzy, evocative soundtrack, I knew I was in the presence of something truly extraordinary.

The painstaking detail in the animations, the vibrant color pallette, and the fluidity of motion create an aesthetic experience that is both nostalgic and refreshingly original. It is as if Studio MDHR has breathed new life into a lost art form, bringing it into the realm of interactive entertainment with unparalleled authenticity.

Cuphead's gameplay is a brilliant fusion of classic run-and-gun mechanics and intricate boss battles. It demands precision, patience and preserverence. Each encounter is a test of skill and timing, a choreography of chaos that challenges the player to adapt and overcome. The difficulty is unforgiving yet fair, ad delicate balance that enusres every victory feels earned and every defeat a lesson in persistence.
